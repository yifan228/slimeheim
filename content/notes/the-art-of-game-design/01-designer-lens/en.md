## The Game Designer's Challenge

How do you create something that allows every player who interacts with it to arrive at a specific experience? Experience is notoriously difficult to share.

## The Solution: Listening

Listening is the most important skill in game design.

- How does it make you feel?
- What does it make you think?
- What does it make you do?
- What is the essential experience?

Listen to: **your team, your audience, your game, your client**, and **yourself**.

## On Essential Experience

Every memorable experience has certain defining qualities that make it unique. The designer's goal is to identify those essential qualities — only then can the experience you want to create truly come to life.

> For example: how do you convey the feeling of "I'm so cold" in a game? Do you show the character's breath turning to mist, or do you add the sound of howling wind?

Find the essential experience and make it the core of your game.

## Disciplines That Inform Game Design

- **Psychology** (leaning toward phenomenology)
- **Cultural Anthropology**
- **Design**

Build on the foundations of psychology and anthropology, then weave in design principles to connect them all.
